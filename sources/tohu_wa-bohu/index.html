Imbued with Zeal

Day 1

Why did you create me with a love for technology? Why do I yearn to build software?

Why do I desire to form and create things with my hands that become idols? (e.g. computer things). Why did you make me this way?

His answer was swift: “I imbued you with zeal for me,” said the Lord.

My true desires – those deep in my soul – are not to worship that which I or others create. No, my desire is to worship you, Jesus. To have zeal for the work You are doing. This merely expresses itself as creations for your Kingdom, but the zeal is for You and from You.

“Of what value is an idol carved by a craftsman? Or an image that teaches lies?

For the one who makes it trusts in his own creation; he makes idols that cannot speak.

Woe to him who says to wood, ‘Come to life!’ or to lifeless stone, ‘Wake up!’

Can it give guidance? It is covered with gold and silver; there is no breath in it.”

  • Habakkuk 2:18 - 19 (NIV)

Created things in our world (I’m looking at you, Alexa) may be able to speak, they may be able to give guidance. The lifeless stone of silicon is indeed imbued with electricity, but it has not awoken. It remains without breath. There is no life in these creations.

For Your creation works through power under. By submitting ourselves to your power.

Consider Romans.

Paul begins by condemning the “other” (e.g. the wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people 1:18 and [they] worshiped and served created things rather than the Creator 1:25).

But then immediately in chapter 2 –  Paul flips the script. Quote: [..] at whatever point you judge another, you are condemning yourself (2:1)

I shall not judge those who worship idols or created things.

For while His invisible attributes [...] have been clearly seen (1:20), who am I to judge?

My zeal for you need be my focus, not created things nor those who worship them. 

A Patient Space

Day 2

The lesson for this day was learned in life with the Spirit preparing it before it sank in. He said “patience as a way of God yields repentance.”

“Abram believed the Lord, and he credited it to him as righteousness.”

My consciousness – with or without You – will bear witness to my heart. In your patience with me, you create wide spaces for me to wander. To have my disobedience roam separate from the soul you created in me. My soul is for you. My sin; however, is sent to the wilderness as a scapegoat. It is in my trusting of You (and your processes) that I am redeemed. That I am credited righteousness – or rightness – back with You. Thank you Lord for creating wide open wilderness for the sin in me to tucker out. To bear witness and learn, so slowly, to come back to You.

You create wide open spaces in your patience to endow me with Your patience and to send the wickedness from me (and also from You).

Day 2: Backstory

The backstory of this day was potent. Receiving a clear word from the Spirit and then going into my day, not realizing I had yet to be tested and fail.

Someone at work took a car charger that I had parked in but had trouble with the app. They remotely reserved it before I could plug in my car. I was low on power. I needed a charge. But they refused to give me the charger. Or rather they did not immediately give it up. I lost my patience. My anger flared and I stormed off.

Later that day, I realized this person was on my team. I was their boss’s boss. How humiliating for me! What kind of leader am I to be so arrogantly angry over such triviality?

Thankfully, the Lord created a spacious place to confront me with my anger and my impatience. To send out the sin in my heart and to bring me back from the wilderness.

I apologized to this person. We were redeemed in some small way. Perhaps never the same, but put right.

If I learned anything from this it was that I have an immense responsibility to bear the name of the Lord with patience.

It is Your Spirit that endows me with that patience. I cannot do it on my own.

But I must seek You and Your Spirit each and every day. Each and every moment. That is my responsibility. Not just for Your sake, for being righteous in your eyes, but – in many ways more importantly – for the sake of others. For the sake of my neighbors.

I need your Spirit not just for its fruits but for its power to bring heaven on earth.

Where Your followers do not storm off like children when they don’t get a car charger.

(It sounds so dumb writing it down, but there it is).

From Above to Please Above

Day 3

“Am I now trying to win the approval of human beings, or of God? Or am I trying to please people? If I were still trying to please people, I would not be a servant of Christ.

I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that the gospel I preached is not of human origin. I did not receive it from any man, nor was I taught it; rather, I received it by revelation from Jesus Christ. ”

  • Galatians 1:10-12

“All they asked was that we should continue to remember the poor, the very thing I had been eager to do all along.”

  • Galatians 2:10

The very instruction and good news that Paul received was not taught to him by people.

If the origin of his revelation on the road to Damascus came from above, then the flow and consequence is for the vessel (e.g. Paul) to turn back to the same origin – to please above. Paul seeks to serve Christ, to please Christ.

But clearly this is not done (in Paul’s estimation) by works. It is not through the Adamic Self-Justification that one can attribute the rightness of your soul by doing things.

The Lord automatically loves. He automatically pours out his Spirit. He has chosen us, we have not chosen Him. There is nothing we can do to earn our way to Him. He comes to us.

And yet.

We aim to please! How can we not! This is an act of worship, not atonement. I love you, Lord and wish to please you and listen to you and obey you. Even in this journey of reflections – it is for you! The words are not even for me, let alone any other audience.

This particular act is one of devotion in daily writing, not daily consumption.

And yet.

Paul reminds us in our aim to please God, we should continue to remember the poor. The greek word πτωχός (ptōchos) is used for poor in this verse. It originates from a root that means “to crouch” or “to cower.” This word is different than the Greek penēs. Penēs is someone who has to work for a living. But ptōchos are those who are utterly destitute and unable to survive. They are in a state of complete helplessness and dependency.

Day 2: Continues

Blessed are they.

I must continue to remember them & the Lord’s Spirit – revealed by Himself and in my desire to please that same Spirit – I will then be able to love them. Not as works, but a reflexive action of love towards my neighbor.

There are 9 fruits of the Spirit. But these are fruits of the Spirit – the things which the Spirit produces, not me. My goal is not to seek these fruits. My goal is to abide in the Lord as my vine. To seek first His kingdom and righteousness which comes through faith. His Spirit then produces within me its own fruits as it sees fit.

His Personal Agency

Day 3

Before beginning this reflection and without remembering what it was, I heard a talk from Dallas Willard titled “Life in the Spirit” from Wheaton College. In this talk, Dallas defined Spirit as: “unembodied personal power”

After experience this and upon review of this day’s reflection (80 days prior) I saw I had written at the top of the card: “HS [Holy Spirit] is an AGENT[,] He has AGENCY”

The Holy Spirit is reliable and a seal of hope. We feel the Spirit (“unembodied personal power”) of the Lord Jesus. He (the Holy Spirit) is an assurance of the hope we have. A guarantee. Our hope rests upon the unembodied personal power of the Spirit of Jesus to act, have agency, and reign in our lives and this world.

The Greek dikaiosune (δικαιοσύνη) is walking in right step with the Lord. Having faith in His agency, in His spirit. Believing, trusting, and our own embodied acting in the world with the trust that His unembodied personal power is acting. Dikaiosune is: (1) believing who He says He is and (2) knowing He will do what he says He will do.

If we believe in Jesus, we act as if He is doing the things He has said He will do. We walk and behave in life as if it were true.

Why do you not practice prayer? Why do you not dedicate your morning or day to His teachings? You do not need to do these things. Doing these things does not give dikaiosune. But instead: believing who He says He is (which is dikaiosune), you will do these things. “If you love me, you will keep my commandments” – but our focus is on His Love. The Holy Spirit is the personal power of Jesus that seals for us His love in our daily walk.

Glory in our sufferings because we know that suffering produces perseverance, and character and hope. Hope does NOT put us to shame because God’s love has poured into our hearts through the Holy Spirit whom He has given us.

Day 3: Personal Reflection

“You will create life”

I do not understand this word, but I heard it from You clear as day.

It was when I contemplated my desire to build material things. A desire to be the one to help create AGI. And Your Spirit spoke loudly as I stared out on the pond outside of BV100. As I peered on the water, the grass, the plants, and birds.

“You will create life”

How? You, Lord, create life. This promise was separate from any building. It was growth. It perhaps was rooted in the imagination of “the Kingdom of Heaven is like a farmer who plants seeds, goes to bed, wakes up and has a harvest.”

It was a reminder not to strive and toil. For the birds do not toil and they are fed. But still.

“You will create life”

How? I still have yet to understand this. Are we, your church, in our embodied weakened state creating life in ourselves and others simply by abiding in you? Is the branch “creating life” by being part of the vine? For the fruit is not my focus. Abiding is.

Does resting in the unembodied personal power of your Holy Spirit (which is one in the same of abiding), help stir up and “create life”?

You give life. I receive life from you.

Perhaps, in abiding, in walking, in faith… we create life. For as the branch are we not part of the vine? If the branch continues to abide in the vine it plugs in and completes the process of life it was meant for.

I may “create life” but it is only through abiding in you.

How Much More

Day 4

“If you, then, though you are evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father in heaven give good gifts to those who ask him!”

  • Matthew 7:11

The death of Adam (that is the choice to attribute the outcomes of humanity’s actions to its own works – visa vie “self-justification”) created a gap between us and God. The infamous – and often tritely quoted – chasm.

Jesus is not just a bridge across this chasm. Not just heaven coming down. The eternal-type of life (which is to say: our knowing of Him) is so much more than the chasm.

You do not merely fill this chasm, Lord. You do not merely bridge this chasm, you overflow it with your grace!

We (our fleshy selves) are buried with your son in this chasm via baptism. Your great inheritance is kept in heaven until the coming of salvation. Until it is ready to be revealed.

I marvel at not just your redemptive power to give good gifts but the magnitude of how magnificently good that grace actually is.

It bridges every gap. It can cross every sea. Neither life nor death can separate us from your love. There is no misstep, mistake or wrong that your power cannot overcome.

But we must seek it. You must seek you. We must seek your kingdom. Your grace – being a good and mighty thing – is not thrust upon us. No, you respect us as a Father respects His children.

How much more are your good gifts that we can merely choose and accept them.

Forgive me for casting your grace – your chasm overflowing grace – aside for other things. I do not want them even if I am stuck with them due to my past poor choices.

Let my fleshy self be buried with you so that I may know the full measure of your love.

Appendix

Embrace, Endure, Elevate

It is only our need for Adamic self justification, and our association of it with the Empire's Kafkaesque bureaucracies that hold us from the potential future that God is offering.

The birth of technology, the hope for an eschatological end times King, and the promise of His Spirit with us has always been available.

God‘s approach to addressing this lack of ours is to give up on the Empire, to give up on the Kafka bureaucracies and embrace instead His future now. 

A future that I believe technology plays a vital part in. For what is technology if not, a generational gift from creation itself that He Himself has created? 

Organic life exploded and left behind us oil. Our predecessors left us knowledge and infrastructure (trains, roads, political and economic systems) to build further upon. Life for humanity does seem to be “progressing” and improving.

for it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose.

  • Philippians 2:13

It is not I nor any of my peers nor anyone living today that can count or take credit for the use of energy infrastructure. For engines and the technology we depend on to build the next layer. Each of these passing phases, like sediment, forming in rock layers creates a stack that he is directing. Towards… something. If we believe His word and that He is the word we must trust that this process will result in His Kingdom coming.

This is not like Babel. It is not us directing this world transformation – at least not as a single undertaking or grandiose project. Our world is God’s work that he continues to refine.

But how do we change other hearts and minds? What is the strategy for how we can address this view (that the Kingdom is coming and we can participate in its building) within ourselves and others?

To do that we must look to Jesus. For His approach was to be with us. To dive down deep into the muck of  human existence. To get close to what we are and what we have chosen to do and what He wants us to become. And to gently and lovingly and humbly point us in a new direction. He worked bottoms up, always.

He never expected this to be a disruptive transformation in a short period of time. No, he called the Kingdom a mustard seed whose disruption comes in the fullness of time.

In God‘s own time. Therefore, if we are to take the layers of technology that He is generationally giving to us, we must approach our deployment of such technologies into human society in the same way that Jesus does.

With humility by and by diving deep into the muck and mire of the Kafka-esque bureaucracies that we’ve created. It is not good enough nor effective enough to simply say that the bureaucracies are broken and must be thrown away. To do that would be to include in the throwing all the individuals, humans, hearts, minds, and souls that we are trying to redeem! No, instead we must embrace, endure and elevate, but through His Spirit.

Embrace the worldly systems as they are. Embrace here means like the prodigal Father embracing his prodigal son. An immediate welcoming and invitation. Embrace here is NOT a support or condoning of Empire’s systems. No. It is treating the lady at the DMV as a sister & image bearer. It is knowing that even the managers and executives are flawed humans who have been gifted their positions and need Empire to break just as much as we do.

Endure is to patiently sacrifice every day. The broken systems that create human injustice must be endured. For in our own endurance, we put to shame the powers and principalities who think His Spirit could be broken by such evil. To Endure is to condemn the system. We must still call out the injustice and be a prophetic witness of Empire’s evil, but we do that through our endurance of it. (And of course outward statements as well). I’ve come to belief that simply calling out the wrong and injustice while lamenting and patiently enduring is the “punk rock” work that God wants from us. For He will change the systems. He is the savior who will crush the serpent’s head. We are but to endure the serpent’s biting while lamenting it is happening. Again, this is not to say there is no action. We can subvert Empire while being submissive. But our focus being on Jesus will enable us to endure it.

Elevate is the open breath of cold fresh air the world needs to have. Elevate is to remember that we follow a resurrected God. Who after putting on the lowest form of humanity to show us love and sacrifice, died. But that death was not the end, not even close. His resurrection is the breath of a new creation. The firstfruits of His Spirit working in the world to bring new life. We are called to elevate the human experience. To stand apart. To be holy. To be an outpost sign in a war that has already been won. To echo that His Kingdom has come and is coming and we can seek and be in it now. That his Kingship is above all other powers and principalities that we may find in this world.

Logo Generation

I generated a logo, but Gemini inserted some characters. This seemed odd, but it was cool what they meant.

These are Japanese Kanji, which are characters originating from Chinese script.

The symbols are read from top to bottom:

  1. 大 (dai / oo-): This character means "big," "large," or "great."
  2. 采 (sai / to-ru): This character means "to pick," "to gather," or "to collect" (like gathering flowers or fruit). It can also carry the nuance of "appearance" or "splendor."
  3. 開 (kai / hira-ku): This character means "to open," "to begin," or "to develop."

When combined as 大采開, the phrase doesn't form a standard, common idiom in Japanese or Chinese. Instead, it appears to be a creative or poetic combination designed for the logo.

Given the image of a hiker exploring a vast landscape, the most likely interpretation is a concept that combines the individual meanings, such as:

  • "Greatly opening up to gather experiences."
  • "Opening to the great splendor (of nature)."
  • "The grand beginning of a collection (of adventures)."

Essentially, the phrase evokes a sense of adventure, exploration, and opening oneself up to discover the grandeur of the world.
